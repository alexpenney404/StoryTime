Bellflower rumpled through the dingy wood, picking odd meta-berries and humming a discordant dirge. Unbeknownst to her, the whauluramp listened to her footfalls from his burrow beneath the fallen leaves. The leaves of summer were dead on the ground, the trees bare and gray. Overhead the sky was bright and cold. A lone balloon drifted high above and off to the East, swimming slowly this way and that to cut against the wind.
